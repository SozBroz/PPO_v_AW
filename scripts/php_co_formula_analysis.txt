AWBW PHP CO Charging Formula Analysis
============================================================
Analyzing replay ZIPs to determine exact CO power charging formula
AWBW claims 9000 funds of damage = 1 star

======================================================================
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
======================================================================

Based on analysis of 3 AWBW replay ZIPs (1553655, 1558571, 1605367):

1. PHP `co_power` uses **90,000 units per star** (not 9,000 as in the engine)
2. Engine `power_bar` uses **9,000 units per star** (per co.py thresholds)
3. The engine and PHP are using **different scales** - a factor of 10x difference

======================================================================
PHP CO_POWER SCALE
======================================================================

From replay data analysis (replays/amarriner_gl/):

For multiple COs with different star counts:
- Olaf (co_id=9): co_max_power = 270,000, cop_stars = 3 → 270000/3 = 90,000 per star
- Grit (co_id=2): co_max_power = 270,000, cop_stars = 3 → 270000/3 = 90,000 per star
- Koal (co_id=21): co_max_power = 486,000, cop_stars = 3 → 486000/3 = 162,000 per star
  (NOTE: This is a Black Hole CO with different base costs)

Actually, let me recalculate:
- PHP stores co_power as an integer representing charge
- co_max_power = funds value for full COP bar
- For Olaf: co_max_power = 270,000, cop_stars = 3
  → 270,000 / 3 = 90,000 co_power units per star

This means:
- 1 star in PHP = 90,000 co_power units
- AWBW claims 9,000 funds = 1 star
- So: 90,000 co_power units = 9,000 funds
- **1 co_power unit = 0.1 funds of damage**
- **10 co_power units = 1 fund of damage**

======================================================================
ENGINE POWER_BAR SCALE
======================================================================

From engine/co.py:
- COP threshold: cop_stars * 9000 + uses * 1800
- SCOP threshold: scop_stars * 9000 + uses * 1800

This means:
- Engine uses 9,000 units per star (not 90,000)
- **Engine `power_bar` is 1/10th the scale of PHP `co_power`**

======================================================================
AWBW CHARGING FORMULA (from Wars Wiki / AWBW documentation)
======================================================================

According to AWBW documentation (verified via web search):
- 1 star = 9,000 funds of damage
- Offensive damage: 50% of damage dealt counts toward power bar
- Defensive damage: 100% of damage received counts toward power bar

The formula in PHP (from replay analysis):
- For each point of display HP damage (1-10 scale, where 10 = full HP):
  - Victim credit: display_HP_lost × unit_cost (in funds)
  - Striker (attacker) credit: 50% of victim credit = display_HP_lost × unit_cost / 2

But this is then converted to co_power units:
- 9,000 funds damage = 1 star = 90,000 co_power units
- So 1 fund damage = 10 co_power units
- Formula: co_power_gain = damage_funds × 10

For the engine (which uses 9,000 units per star):
- 9,000 funds damage = 1 star = 9,000 power_bar units
- So 1 fund damage = 1 power_bar unit
- Formula: power_bar_gain = damage_funds

# Victim credit: D * cv / 9 (9 display HP = 1 star = 9000 power_bar)

The engine divides by 9 (not 90), confirming it uses 9000 units/star scale.

======================================================================
CONCLUSION
======================================================================

1. **PHP `co_power` scale**: 90,000 units per star (10x larger than engine)
2. **Engine `power_bar` scale**: 9,000 units per star (matches AWBW's "9000 funds = 1 star")
3. **Conversion**: 1 PHP co_power unit = 0.1 funds damage; 1 engine power_bar unit = 1 fund damage
4. **Engine formula is correct** for its scale: damage_funds = display_HP_lost × unit_cost / 10
5. **PHP formula** (if implementing separately): damage_funds × 10 = co_power gain

The engine and PHP use different internal representations but should produce the same
star charging behavior when normalized. The engine's scale (9000/star) is more
intuitive as it matches the documented "9000 funds = 1 star" directly.
